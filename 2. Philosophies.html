Homeschoool Philosophies

You might be thinking to yourself, "I am ready to home school!". You probably are right!
One issue we ran into as a family was once we decided to homeschool we uncovered tons of resources. Like An overwhekming amount. So, I would consider this page as a step 1. Before you even begin ordering curriculum and meeting with your ed specialist* I would do a dive into philosophy. Youll notice this is not "how to" but more of the why which direcet the how.

Classical Approach

  • Focus: Emphasizes the trivium (grammar, logic, rhetoric) as a foundation for learning.
  • Structure: History-centered curriculum, integrating subjects chronologically.
  • Goal: Develop critical thinking, eloquence, and a love for classical literature.

Charlotte Mason

  • Focus: Learning through "living books" instead of textbooks, emphasizing narration and nature studies.
  • Structure: Short lessons to maintain focus, with arts and outdoor exploration central.
  • Goal: Nurture a child's character, curiosity, and appreciation for beauty.

Montessori

  • Focus: Child-led learning with hands-on activities in a prepared environment.
  • Structure: Encourages independence, self-paced exploration, and discovery.
  • Goal: Foster intrinsic motivation and holistic development.

Christian

  • Focus: Child-directed learning based on interests rather than a formal curriculum.
  • Structure: No fixed schedules or traditional methods; relies on real-world experiences.
  • Goal: Promote lifelong learning and curiosity through organic exploration.

Unit Studies

  • Focus: Interdisciplinary learning where one topic integrates multiple subjects.
  • Structure: Thematic approach, e.g., studying "oceans" could cover science, geography, art, and literature.
  • Goal: Make learning cohesive and engaging by connecting knowledge to central themes.
